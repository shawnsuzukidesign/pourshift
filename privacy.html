Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 25, 2026
Last updated: May 25, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how PourShift ("PourShift," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, discloses, and protects information when you visit our website, join the waitlist, create an account, or otherwise use the PourShift platform (the "Service").

By using the Service, you agree to the practices described here. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

1. Who we are

PourShift is a connection platform that helps hospitality venues discover bartenders and barbacks in the San Francisco Bay Area. PourShift is not a staffing agency, recruiter, or employer of record. Any hiring relationship is between the venue and the worker, PourShift does not employ, pay, schedule, or manage workers.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Information you provide

  • Account & profile data, name, email, phone number, city, role (bartender, barback, venue operator), years of experience, specialties, and similar profile details.
  • Venue data, venue name, address, contact person, roles you are hiring for, and related business information.
  • Credentials, California Responsible Beverage Service (RBS) certification, food handler permits, city-specific permits, expiration dates, and uploaded certificate images or documents.
  • Skill content, short pour videos, photos, and other media you upload to demonstrate technique.
  • Communications, messages, support requests, and feedback you send to us or exchange through the Service.
  • Payment information. When venues pay invite fees, hire-confirmation fees, or subscription fees, and when bartenders subscribe to the cert-priority badge, our payment processor (Stripe) collects the card details. PourShift does not store full card numbers on our servers.
  • Payout information for bartenders. To pay out hire bonuses we may collect bank/ACH details or an email address for gift card delivery.

2.2 Information collected automatically

  • Usage data, pages viewed, features used, time spent, referring URLs, and similar analytics.
  • Device data, IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers, and approximate location derived from IP.
  • Cookies and similar technologies, see Section 7 below.

2.3 Information from third parties

We may receive information from identity verification services, certification issuers, payment processors, and analytics providers to confirm credentials, prevent fraud, and operate the Service.

3. How we use information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Create and operate your account and profile;
  • Help venues discover and connect with bartenders and barbacks, including reclassifying workers as barbacks when their certifications expire;
  • Verify certifications and send renewal reminders;
  • Charge venues for invite and hire-confirmation fees and subscriptions, and pay out bartender hire bonuses via ACH or gift card;
  • Provide customer support and respond to inquiries;
  • Send transactional emails (e.g., confirmations, invite notifications, certification reminders) and, with your consent where required, marketing emails;
  • Detect, investigate, and prevent fraud, abuse, and violations of our Terms of Use;
  • Improve and develop new features;
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our agreements.

4. How we share information

We share information only as described below.

4.1 Between bartenders and venues

Profile information, pour videos, certification status, and city are visible to venues browsing the platform. Last name, contact information, and full certification details are revealed to a venue only after the venue pays the invite fee for that worker. Venues that receive contact details may use them only to contact the worker about the role identified in the invite.

4.2 Service providers

We share information with vendors that help us run the Service, including:

  • Supabase, database and authentication hosting;
  • Stripe, payment processing for venue charges;
  • ACH and gift card providers, to pay bartender hire bonuses;
  • Netlify, website hosting;
  • Email delivery, analytics, and customer support tools.

These providers may access information only to perform services on our behalf and are contractually required to protect it.

4.3 Legal and safety

We may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, or court order, or if we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of PourShift, our users, or the public.

4.4 Business transfers

If PourShift is involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to standard confidentiality terms.

4.5 With your consent

We share information for other purposes with your consent.

We do not sell your personal information for money. See Section 8 for how California law treats certain sharing for cross-context behavioral advertising.

5. Data retention

We retain personal information for as long as your account is active and for a reasonable period after to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Pour videos and certification records may be retained while your profile is published. You can request deletion as described in Section 8.

6. Security

We use technical and organizational measures designed to protect personal information, including encryption in transit (HTTPS), access controls, and a strict Content Security Policy on our website. No system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Cookies and tracking

We use a limited set of cookies and similar technologies for authentication, security, and basic analytics. You can control cookies through your browser settings. Blocking essential cookies may prevent parts of the Service from working.

8. Your privacy rights

8.1 California residents (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

  • Know what personal information we collect and how we use and share it;
  • Request a copy of your personal information;
  • Request deletion of your personal information;
  • Request correction of inaccurate personal information;
  • Opt out of "sale" or "sharing" of personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising (PourShift does not currently sell personal information);
  • Be free from retaliation for exercising these rights.

To exercise these rights, email support@pourshift.com. We will verify your request using information already associated with your account.

8.2 Other jurisdictions

Residents of other states or countries may have similar rights under local law. Contact us at the address above and we will respond as required by applicable law.

9. Children

The Service is intended for users 21 years of age or older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18. If you believe a minor has provided us information, contact us and we will delete it.

10. International users

PourShift operates in the United States. If you access the Service from outside the U.S., your information will be transferred to and processed in the U.S., which may have different data protection laws than your country.

11. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date above. Material changes will be communicated by email or a notice on the Service before they take effect.

12. Contact us

Questions about this Policy or our privacy practices? Email support@pourshift.com.
